CONSTITUTION OF TUARA KURI LIMITED
Adopted on: 2026-04-09

1. Name
The name of the company is TUARA KURI LIMITED.

2. Registered Office
The registered office of the company is at the address determined by the director from time to time and notified to the Registrar of Companies in accordance with the Companies Act 1993.

3. Share Capital
3.1 Authorised shares
The company is authorised to issue a maximum of 100 shares.

3.2 Rights attaching to shares
Each share confers on the holder:

(a) One vote on a poll at any meeting of shareholders;

(b) The right to receive an equal share of any dividend declared by the director;

(c) The right to receive an equal share of the surplus assets of the company on a liquidation, after payment of all creditors.

3.3 Issue of shares
Subject to the Companies Act 1993, the director may issue shares at any time, on any terms, and for any consideration (including cash, property, or services) that the director determines.

3.4 No pre-emptive rights
No shareholder has any pre-emptive or other right to acquire shares issued by the company.

4. Directors
4.1 Number of directors
The company shall have one director. The sole director may appoint additional directors by resolution.

4.2 First director
Kade Cowper is appointed as the first director of the company.

4.3 Powers of the director
Subject to the Companies Act 1993, the director has full and unrestricted power to manage the business and affairs of the company, including without limitation:

(a) Opening and operating bank accounts;

(b) Entering into contracts, agreements, and undertakings on behalf of the company;

(c) Appointing and removing employees, contractors, and agents;

(d) Borrowing money, giving security, and issuing guarantees;

(e) Issuing shares;

(f) Declaring and paying dividends;

(g) Acquiring, disposing of, or encumbering assets of the company.

4.4 Director remuneration
The director may receive reasonable remuneration for services provided to the company, as determined by the director.

4.5 Director's interest
The director may hold any office or have any interest in any other entity, even if that entity competes with the company. The director is not required to account to the company for any benefit received from such office or interest.

4.6 Indemnity and insurance
(a) The company indemnifies the director to the fullest extent permitted by the Companies Act 1993 for any liability incurred by the director in the performance of their duties.

(b) The company may purchase and maintain insurance for the director against any liability.

5. Shareholder Meetings
5.1 No annual meeting required
The company is not required to hold an annual general meeting.

5.2 Calling of meetings
(a) The director may call a meeting of shareholders at any time.

(b) A shareholder holding 5% or more of the voting shares may request in writing that the director call a meeting. The director must call the meeting within 20 working days of receiving the request.

5.3 Notice of meetings
Notice of any meeting of shareholders must be given in writing at least 10 working days before the meeting, unless the director determines a shorter period is reasonable.

5.4 Quorum
The quorum for a meeting of shareholders is one shareholder present in person or by proxy.

5.5 Voting
(a) On a show of hands, each shareholder present in person has one vote.

(b) On a poll, each shareholder has one vote for each share held.

5.6 Written resolutions
A resolution in writing signed by all shareholders entitled to vote on that resolution is as valid and effective as if passed at a meeting of shareholders.

6. Dividends
The director may declare and pay dividends at any time and in any amount, provided the company satisfies the solvency test in section 4 of the Companies Act 1993 (meaning the company must be able to pay its debts as they become due in the normal course of business, and the value of the company's assets must exceed its liabilities).

7. Accounts and Records
The director must ensure the company maintains, in accordance with the Companies Act 1993:

(a) Accounting records that correctly record the transactions of the company;

(b) A share register showing all shares issued, transfers, and cancellations;

(c) Minutes of all meetings and resolutions of the director and shareholders.

8. Financial Reporting
The company is not required to prepare annual financial statements or have them audited under the Companies Act 1993, unless:

(a) The director decides otherwise; or

(b) The company becomes a "large company" under the Financial Reporting Act 2013 (generally, annual operating expenditure exceeding $10 million).

If financial statements are prepared, they need not be audited unless required by law or the director decides otherwise.

9. Winding Up
If the company is liquidated, the surplus assets remaining after payment of all debts and liabilities shall be distributed to the shareholders in proportion to their shareholdings.

10. Amendments
The director may amend this constitution by resolution. Any amendment takes effect immediately, unless the resolution specifies a later date.

11. Governing Law
This constitution is governed by and interpreted in accordance with the laws of New Zealand.

12. Definitions
In this constitution, unless the context otherwise requires:

"Companies Act 1993" means the Companies Act 1993 (New Zealand), as amended from time to time.

"Director" means the director of the company, acting by resolution.

"Shareholder" means a person whose name is entered in the share register as a holder of shares.

Adoption
This constitution was adopted by the director of TUARA KURI LIMITED on the date first written above.
